How to Measure Your Feet

1

Prepare Your Materials

Get a piece of paper, a pen or pencil, a ruler or measuring tape, and wear the socks you'd normally wear with sneakers.

2

Trace Your Foot

Place the paper on a hard floor against a wall. Stand on the paper with your heel against the wall and trace around your foot, keeping the pen perpendicular to the paper.

3

Measure the Length

Measure from the back of your heel to the tip of your longest toe. This is your foot length in centimeters or inches.

4

Find Your Size

Use the measurement to find your size in the charts below. If you're between sizes, we recommend going up to the next size for comfort.

Pro Tip: Measure your feet at the end of the day when they're slightly larger. Also, measure both feet as one may be slightly bigger than the other.

Brand-Specific Fit Guide

Nike

Generally runs true to size. Air Max models may run slightly narrow. Consider going half size up for wide feet.

Jordan

Jordan 1s run true to size. Jordan 4s tend to run slightly large - consider going half size down. Jordan 11s fit snug.

Nike Dunks

Run true to size for most people. SB Dunks have extra padding and may feel snug initially but break in nicely.

Vans

Run true to size. Old Skools and Authentics have a roomier fit. Sk8-His may feel slightly narrow.

Converse

Chuck Taylors run large - go down a full size from your usual. Chuck 70s fit more true to size.

Air Force 1

Run about half size large. Most people go down half a size for the best fit.
